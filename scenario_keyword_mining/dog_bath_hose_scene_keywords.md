## Hook Hypotheses

Each hook idea must include a business purpose, metric impact, and first-principles reason.

### Muddy Paws Hook

- Hook idea: "Muddy paws do not have to hit your clean floor."
- Business purpose: Position the hose as a fast cleanup tool after walks.
- Metric impact: Improve 3-second retention by showing an instantly recognizable problem.
- First-principles reason: Viewers stop when the opening scene matches a recent, concrete pain.

### Bath Time Mess Hook

- Hook idea: "Bath time should not turn into a wet floor cleanup."
- Business purpose: Sell convenience and cleanup reduction.
- Metric impact: Improve completion and product clicks by linking the demo to a home-care pain.
- First-principles reason: A product demo works better when the viewer understands the before/after job.

### Dog Refuses Bath Hook

- Hook idea: "If your dog hates baths, start with a quicker rinse."
- Business purpose: Frame the product as a lower-friction grooming step.
- Metric impact: Improve comments and saves from owners with bath-resistant dogs.
- First-principles reason: Emotional relevance rises when the hook names a common owner struggle.

## Demo Logic

- Show messy dog or muddy paws.
- Show hose setup or water flow control.
- Show quick rinse motion.
- Show cleaner paws, floor, or outdoor cleanup.
- End with a simple at-home grooming benefit.

## Differentiated Selling Points

- Faster rinse after muddy walks.
- Less bathroom mess.
- More controlled water flow.
- Useful outdoors before the dog enters the home.
- Clear before/after cleanup story.
